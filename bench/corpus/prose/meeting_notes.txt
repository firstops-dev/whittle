Engineering Sync Meeting Notes - July 3, 2024, 10:00 AM PDT

Attendees: Sarah Chen (Engineering Lead), Michael Rodriguez (Backend Team), Jessica Wong (Frontend Team), James Park (Database Team), Robert Kim (DevOps), Emma Wilson (QA Lead)

Agenda Items

1. Post-Incident Review (15 min)
Sarah opened with a summary of yesterday's database failover incident. The team reviewed the timeline and preliminary root causes that were documented in the postmortem. Michael asked about the specific query that caused the issue. James confirmed it was a customer-generated report that was not properly scoped. This led to a discussion about implementing query timeouts at the application level.

2. Q3 Planning (30 min)
Jessica presented the frontend roadmap items for Q3. The team is prioritizing dashboard redesign and performance optimizations. Michael raised concerns about the complexity of the redesign and suggested breaking it into smaller chunks. The team agreed to schedule a detailed planning session next week to finalize the breakdown.

Robert raised the topic of infrastructure reliability. Given yesterday's incident, he proposed dedicating engineering time to infrastructure hardening. Sarah agreed this was important and suggested allocating 20% of team bandwidth in Q3 to infrastructure improvements. The team seemed receptive to this approach.

3. Technical Debt Review (20 min)
Emma highlighted several areas where technical debt has accumulated. The Kubernetes manifests are outdated and need to be updated to use the latest best practices. The team's internal RPC framework needs optimization because it is becoming a bottleneck for inter-service communication. Sarah suggested creating a technical debt backlog and triaging items based on impact and effort.

James mentioned the database schema has not been updated since early 2023. There are several migrations that have been queued but not deployed. Sarah asked James to prioritize these and present a deployment plan at next week's meeting.

4. Security Review (15 min)
Michael brought up a security concern with the current authentication implementation. The team is currently storing session tokens in cookies without proper SameSite protection. This could expose users to CSRF attacks. Jessica asked if there was a timeline to fix this. Michael estimated the fix would take one sprint and suggested starting in Q3. Sarah approved this and marked it as a P1 task.

Robert asked if the team had conducted a recent security audit. Sarah replied that the last audit was six months ago and suggested scheduling a new one for Q3. The team agreed to prioritize this.

5. Hiring Updates (10 min)
Sarah shared that the team has an open position for a senior backend engineer. The hiring process is in the final stages with two candidates in the offer stage. She also mentioned that a current team member is transitioning to a different team, so the overall team headcount would remain stable. The team expressed concerns about knowledge transfer and Sarah agreed to allocate time for documentation and pairing sessions over the next few weeks.

6. Q2 Retrospective Results (15 min)
Emma summarized the Q2 retrospective findings. The team felt the sprint planning process could be improved to reduce mid-sprint priority changes. The team also expressed frustration with production deployment processes being error-prone. Robert suggested implementing a deployment checklist and automated deployment validation. The team agreed to implement these changes in Q3.
